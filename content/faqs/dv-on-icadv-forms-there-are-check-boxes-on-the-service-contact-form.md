question: On ICADV forms, there are check boxes on the Service Contact form
  under Service Interaction for Contact circuit clerk, Safety/flight planning,
  and Explained court process. Do these have anything to do with the reporting
  of activities to the InfoNet?
answer: >
  The check boxes help save writing time for staff. These are not services
  entered into InfoNet, but common activities. There may be 3 or 4 boxes checked
  for topics covered that fit within one service

  category. For example, you might have spent an hour with a client and check 4 boxes. The hour of service fits civil legal advocacy, which is entered into InfoNet as a service. For each contact you 1) enter the hours of service in appropriate category, 2) indicate common topics covered (check boxes), and 3) write notes for interaction in the Comments section.

The check boxes help save writing time for staff. These are not services entered into InfoNet, but common activities. There may be 3 or 4 boxes checked for topics covered that fit within one service
category. For example, you might have spent an hour with a client and check 4 boxes. The hour of service fits civil legal advocacy, which is entered into InfoNet as a service. For each contact you 1) enter the hours of service in appropriate category, 2) indicate common topics covered (check boxes), and 3) write notes for interaction in the Comments section.
