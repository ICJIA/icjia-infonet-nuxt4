question: May I enter the services provided by contractual consultants or
  providers into the database?
answer: Yes, but first you must enter the contractual person in the
  Administration > Staff Information section of the database using their own
  first and last names. If you want it to be displayed that this is a
  contractual person versus a permanent employee, you may edit that information
  into the client’s first name. For example, if John Smith was hired to do
  contractual work, you might enter his last name as Smith and his first name as
  John (Contractual). If you enter his name this way, it will always be clear to
  whoever is entering or viewing data in InfoNet that this is a contractual
  staff person.

Yes, but first you must enter the contractual person in the Administration > Staff Information section of the database using their own first and last names. If you want it to be displayed that this is a contractual person versus a permanent employee, you may edit that information into the client’s first name. For example, if John Smith was hired to do contractual work, you might enter his last name as Smith and his first name as John (Contractual). If you enter his name this way, it will always be clear to whoever is entering or viewing data in InfoNet that this is a contractual staff person.
